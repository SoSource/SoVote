If you could run your own government as easily as using Facebook,

Would you?


Currently, participation in democracy for most means marking a ballot for one of two or three people, every four years. Thousands of years ago, when democracy was created, that was not the case. At that time, all citizens could vote on any issue. Centuries later when representative democracy was created, it was practical, given the circumstances; they had considerations of geography and expertise.

But they didn't have the Internet, they didn't have smartphones. They didn't have the levels of bloat and corruption that today has.

Today your legislative government consists of two chambers, the Senate and the House. The conditions have come to be for a third: the Citizenry. A chamber that consists of every single voting age citizen and gives them the right to collectively approve or veto any new law.

At SoVote:

We believe by engaging with our government we are exercising and fulfilling our civic duty as citizens of our community and country.

We believe we can save ourselves from the disconnected and corrupt by claiming our own power for ourselves.

We believe if enough of us gather in one place, we will be heard.

This is a place to:

  • Represent yourself from the federal to the municipal levels of government.
  • Debate the merits of any bill or issue.
  • Vote yea or nay on any bill.
  • Write your own bills.
  • Create and participate in polls.

  • Reduce partisan politics by taking power from those who would vote down party lines.
  • Match your voting history to each candidate to see who would best represent your will.

  • Write all votes to a decentralized, immutable blockchain for independant backup and verification.
  • Create a SoVote node for each and every locality.
  • Decentralize Democracy.
